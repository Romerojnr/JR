About Us


The Ministry of Tourism, Arts & Culture (MoTAC) in Ghana is the government ministry responsible for the development and promotion of tourism-related activities in the country. Established in 1993, its functions aim to optimize the socio-economic growth of Ghana through tourism and cultural development.

Functions: Developing and promoting tourism and improving the capital city, Accra. Enhancing the socio-economic benefits of tourism for deprived communities with tourist sites.

Tourism Statistics:

In 2011, Ghana welcomed approximately 1,087,000 tourists. Tourist attractions include wildlife, waterfalls like Kintampo and Tagbo Falls, coastal sandy beaches, caves, mountains, rivers, and lakes such as Lake Bosumtwi and Lake Volta. The country also boasts numerous castles and forts, UNESCO World Heritage Sites, nature reserves, and national parks.

Major Tourist Sites:

  1. Kakum National Park

  2. Mole National Park

  3. Cape Coast Castle

  4. Elmina Castle

  5. Nzulezo

These sites are significant contributors to Ghana's tourism industry and are managed under the purview of MoTAC
