Our Culture

This is an introduction to our amazing webpage. Explore the sections below.

Ancient and Pre-Colonial Era

Ghana's cultural roots date back to powerful ancient kingdoms like the Ghana Empire

(not in modern Ghana but in West Africa), the Mali Empire, and later the Ashanti Empire.

The Ashanti, Fante, Ga, Ewe, and many other ethnic groups developed unique traditions, languages, and social structures.

Traditional religion, drumming, storytelling, and festivals played a crucial role in their society.

Colonial Period (15th–20th Century)

European contact began in the 15th century with the arrival of the Portuguese, followed by the Dutch, British, and others.

The transatlantic slave trade and gold trade influenced Ghana’s history.

The British colonized Ghana (then the Gold Coast) in the 19th century, bringing Western education, Christianity, and European governance systems.

Post-Independence (1957–Present)

Ghana became the first African country to gain independence in 1957, led by Kwame Nkrumah.

Post-independence, Ghana preserved its cultural identity while embracing modern influences.

The country remains known for colorful kente cloth, highlife music,

traditional festivals (like Homowo and Aboakyir), and unique foods like jollof rice and fufu.
